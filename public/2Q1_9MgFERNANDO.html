Background to the 3 Dimensions:

Minecraft has two pocket dimensions completely separate from your peaceful spawn place, the Overworld: there is a land filled boiling lava known as the Nether, and galactic islands floating over a dark void called the End.

The Overworld

Biomes

The Overworld contains a diverse number of biomes such as Taiga Forests, Ice Spikes, Dark Oak Forests, Deserts and Jungles.

Structures

There are also many structures such as Villages, Desert Temples, Igloos, Mansions and Jungle Temples.

Ores

Many resources are mined through ores such as Iron, Diamond, Emerald and Gold.

Bosses/Sub-bosses

While there are not any Main bosses in the Overworld, there are some optional sub-bosses due to their immense strength and high health points. These bosses include:

  1. The Warden

    Found in the Deep Dark is the Warden, a monster who relies on sound to find and destroy the player with high damages smashes and a long ranged sonic boom that pierces through blocks, as well as being very durable.

  2. The Elder Guardian

    On the other hand, the Elder Guardian is found in Ocean Monumments, protecting its treasures of gold blocks. It applies as special status effect - mining fatigue - on the player to reduce their speed in breaking blocks, as well as being able to shoot lasers from its eyes.

OVERWORLD




The Nether

Background on The Nether

The Nether can be accessed by building a nether portal in the overworld, creating a 4 block wide, 5 block tall outline of Obsidian and lighting it with a fire source, whether a fire charge, burning wood, or flint and steel.

Biomes

The Nether also contains a diverse number of biomes such as Warped Forests, Crimson Forests, Basalt Deltas and Soul Sand Valleys.

Structures

Some structures in the Nether include the Bastion Remnants and Nether Fortresses.

Ores

You can find Quartz Ores and Ancient Debris.

Bosses/Sub-bosses

  • The Wither

    By placing 4 blocks of Soul Sand in a 'T' shape, and three Wither Skeleton Skulls at each top block, the player can summon a powerful entity known as the Wither. Having its own health bar, the Wither (in Minecraft Java Edition) can fly and shoots Wither Skull Projectiles at the player that explode on contact. It also has a second phase that makes it invulnerable to projectiles. Upon defeating it, the player will gain a Nether Star and a Wither Rose.

NETHER




The End

Background on The End

The End Portal is accessed through the End Portal located in the Stronghold- underground dungeons with a room containing a 5x5 block portal- where you can place Eyes of Ender (a combination of Ender Pearls and Blaze Powder) to ignite a portal.

Biomes and Ores

Compared to the previous dimensions, the End is quite bare since it only has two biomes- the Outer End and Main End Island, as well as not having any ores.

Structures

The only structure in the end is the End City, floating towers that contain many loot such as diamonds and even an ELytra on an End Ship that allow the player to fly. However, the treasures are all guarded by Shulkers, boxes containing entities that shoot projectiles that make the player float in the air.

Bosses/Sub-bosses

  • The Ender Dragon

    Being the final boss of Minecraft, the Ender Dragon flies around the Main End island, spits fire balls that create a lingering damage effect, and swoops down and physically attacks and launches the player high in the air. There are also End Crystals sitting atop Obsidian pillars that allow it to quickly regenerate its health. After destroying the crystals and defeating the Ender Dragon, it will drop many Experience Points and a Dragon Egg that sits on the Bedrock fountain, waiting to be collected by the player.

SIMILARITIES Each are all very unique 3D spaces with many types of blocks used or crafted to become different resources for the player. Some blocks are affected by gravity such as Gravel and Sand which can be found in the Nether and Overworld, while most other blocks float. The overworld is affected by a day/night cycle, while the Nether and End aren't since they are completely seperate pocket dimensions without a sun and moon. There are many other factors that make each dimension similar and unique, and that's what makes it special: it builds on the resources, mobs and structures each player can find creating a perfec sandbox game!
