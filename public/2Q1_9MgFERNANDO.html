The Three Dimensions of Minecraft

Structures, Ores, Biomes and Bosses

Background to the 3 Dimensions:

Minecraft has two pocket dimensions completely separate from your peaceful spawn place, the Overworld: there is a land filled boiling lava known as the Nether, and galactic islands floating over a dark void called the End.

The Overworld

Biomes

The Overworld contains a diverse number of biomes such as Taiga Forests, Ice Spikes, Dark Oak Forests, Deserts and Jungles.

Structures

There are also many structures such as Villages, Desert Temples, Igloos, Mansions and Jungle Temples.

Ores

Many resources are mined through ores such as Iron, Diamond, Emerald and Gold.

Bosses/Sub-bosses

While there are not any Main bosses in the Overworld, there are some optional sub-bosses due to their immense strength and high health points. These bosses include:

  1. The Warden

  2. The Elder Guardian

The Nether

Background on The Nether

The Nether can be accessed by building a nether portal in the overworld, creating a 4 block wide, 5 block tall outline of Obsidian and lighting it with a fire source, whether a fire charge, burning wood, or flint and steel.

Biomes

The Nether also contains a diverse number of biomes such as Warped Forests, Crimson Forests, Basalt Deltas and Soul Sand Valleys.

Structures

Some structures in the Nether include the Bastion Remnants and Nether Fortresses.

Ores

You can find Quartz Ores and Ancient Debris.

The End

Background on The Nether

The End Portal is accessed through the End Portal located in the Stronghold- underground dungeons with a room containing a 5x5 block portal- where you can place Eyes of Ender (a combination of Ender Pearls and Blaze Powder) to ignite a portal.

Biomes and Ores

Compared to the previous dimensions, the End is quite bare since it only has two biomes- the Outer End and Main End Island, as well as not having any ores.

Structures

Some structures in the Nether include the Bastion Remnants and Nether Fortresses.
